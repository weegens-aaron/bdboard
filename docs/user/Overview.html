bdboard — Overview

A friendly tour of bdboard for people who use it. No code, no file paths — just what bdboard is, what you can do with it, and how the pieces fit together. For step-by-step tasks see the Guides; for the background ideas see the Concepts.

In one sentence

bdboard is a live, always-fresh web dashboard for your beads workspace — it opens in your browser and shows your work as colour-coded swim lanes, with full detail on every item, a history page with trends, and a place to manage the notes your AI agents rely on.

What it's for

If you track your work as beads (small issue cards — tasks, bugs, epics, and so on), bdboard gives you an at-a-glance picture without leaving your terminal-driven workflow. You start it from inside a project, a browser tab opens, and from then on the board keeps itself up to date on its own — you never have to hit refresh. It's a viewer first: it reads your workspace and shows it beautifully, and the few changes it can make (editing a field, adding a note, removing a memory, creating items from a template) only ever happen when you ask for them.

How the pieces fit together

bdboard has three pages — the Board, the History page, and the Memory page — that all look at the same live workspace. When anything in your workspace changes, every open tab quietly refreshes the parts that need it.

A few things worth knowing up front

  • You never refresh. A small "live" indicator shows the board is watching your workspace; changes appear within a second or so. See Concept: ...how it stays current.
  • *The board shows recent finished work; the History page shows all* of it.** The Closed lane on the board is a short rolling window so it stays tidy; to look further back, open History and pick a date range. See Concept: Time ranges & recent work.
  • Your data stays on your machine. bdboard reads your local workspace and shows it to you in your own browser. See Concept: Your data is local & safe.
  • Some beads are read-only on purpose. An item that's actively being worked on (or already finished) can't be edited from the board, so you can't accidentally step on work in progress. See Feature: Bead detail & editing.

What bdboard relies on

It needs… Why
A beads workspace bdboard is a viewer for your beads — it shows the work you track there. New, empty workspaces show an empty board until they have items.
A web browser The whole dashboard runs in your browser; a tab opens automatically when bdboard starts.
Your beads tool bdboard reads your workspace through your beads command-line tool, so that tool needs to be available where you run bdboard.
