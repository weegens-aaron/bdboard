Live updates

What Is It

Live updates mean the board, the History screen, and the Memory screen keep themselves current on their own. When work changes — yours or a teammate's — the pages reflect it within moments, with no reloading and no refresh button.

Why It Matters

A board is only trustworthy if it's true right now. If you had to remember to reload, you'd constantly be looking at a stale picture and second-guessing it. Live updates mean what you see is what's actually happening, so you can leave a board open all day and trust it as your single source of truth — even across several open tabs.

How It Works

Picture a noticeboard with a quiet assistant standing beside it. Whenever anything about the project changes, the assistant swaps the affected card for an up-to-date one — you just see it change.

bdboard works the same way:

  • It watches your project for changes behind the scenes.
  • When something changes, only the affected parts of the page are updated — a card moves lanes, a count ticks up, a memory appears — rather than reloading the whole page.
  • Every open tab gets the same update, so two people (or two windows) never drift out of sync.

Because of this, actions you take in bdboard show their results automatically. Claim an item and it slides from Ready to In Progress; pour a formula and the new items appear; close something and the counts adjust — all without you reloading.

Tip

If a page ever looks frozen, a single manual refresh re-establishes the live connection and updates resume on their own.
